About The Designer

8 Years of Purposeful Interface Motion

Not a generalist. A specialist who has dedicated an entire career to the craft of UI choreography.

Origin

Designer Story

I started my career designing static screen layouts, but rapidly discovered that the real magic — the part that made users feel delight or frustration — happened in the moments between states.

Motion is the body language of digital products. A sluggish modal tells users the app is slow. A spring-physics button says it's alive and responsive. I became obsessed with getting those moments right.

For the past 8 years I've worked exclusively at the intersection of design and interface animation, helping SaaS companies, startups, and enterprise teams make their products feel physically real.

Philosophy

Motion Philosophy

"Motion is the difference between a product that is merely used, and one that is truly loved."

I believe in physics-based design — where digital interfaces obey the same laws of mass and momentum as the physical world. Springs, not linear curves. Resistance, not instant teleportation.

The best interface animation goes unnoticed. Users simply feel that the product is fast, responsive, and trustworthy — without understanding why.

Questions

FAQs

How do you handoff animations to developers? +

Lottie JSON for icon animations, Framer Motion React code snippets for complex interactions, CSS custom properties for timing tokens, and a detailed motion spec doc with all easing curves.

Do you design screens too, or only animate existing ones? +

Both. I can design UI screens from scratch in Figma and then animate them, or work on top of your existing design system to inject motion without disrupting established visual patterns.

What's the minimum engagement size? +

The smallest engagement is typically a micro-interaction audit + 5 custom animations. This usually takes 2 weeks and starts from $2,000.

Do you work with early-stage startups? +

Absolutely. I've worked with pre-seed founders building investor pitch prototypes, all the way to Series C companies standardizing motion across 40+ product screens.
